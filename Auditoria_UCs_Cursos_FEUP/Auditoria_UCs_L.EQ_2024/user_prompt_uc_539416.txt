<div style="text-align: justify;"> Os objetivos gerais são:</div>
<ul>
<li style="text-align: justify;">
<div>Proporcionar um conhecimento adequado dos princípios, instrumentação e aplicações das técnicas analíticas mais frequentes, incluindo a espectroscopia de absorção atómica e molecular, espetrometria de emissão atómica com atomização por indução de plasma, os métodos eletroquímicos e separativos (cromatográficos);</div>
</li>
<li style="text-align: justify;">
<div>Proporcionar um conhecimento adequado dos principais processos de extração e manuseamento de amostras sólidas, líquidas e gasosas e das suas implicações ao nível da incerteza da medição analítica;</div>
</li>
<li style="text-align: justify;">
<div>Proporcionar as capacidades necessárias que permitam ao aluno selecionar uma determinada técnica analítica para resolver um problema, determinar eventuais restrições, selecionar o método analítico mais adequado, identificar alternativas, comparar as vantagens e desvantagens de cada um e exibir capacidade crítica de interpretação de resultados analíticos;</div>
</li>
<li style="text-align: justify;">
<div>Desenvolver as capacidades de comunicação, em particular, de resultados técnicos e as capacidades de cooperação em grupo;</div>
</li>
<li>
<div style="text-align: justify;">Estimular o uso de bases da teoria científica para resolver problemas do mundo real e desenvolver capacidades de pensamento crítico.</div>
</li>
</ul>

## Resultados de aprendizagem e competências
<p style="text-align: left;">Para atingir estes objetivos, os alunos devem saber:</p>
<ul>
<li>Explicar os princípios e modo de funcionamento dos equipamentos analíticos mais usados nas áreas de eletroquímica, espectroscópica e cromatográfica;</li>
<li>Projetar uma experiência analítica para resolver um problema real;</li>
<li>Conduzir uma análise laboratorial, de acordo com as Boas Práticas de Laboratório, em condições de higiene e segurança, que envolva manipulação da amostra, extração, pré-concentração e medição instrumental e calcular o resultado analítico e respetiva incerteza;</li>
<li>Interpretar e comunicar um resultado analítico e produzir relatórios técnicos;</li>
<li>Funcionar eficientemente em equipa.</li>
</ul>

## Pré-requisitos e co-requisitos
Não aplicável

## Programa
<div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;><strong><u>Programa teórico-prático</u>:</strong></div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;><strong>Introdução. </strong>Introdução à Química Analítica e Métodos Instrumentais de Análise. Etapas de um método analítico. Processos de preparação de amostras (LLE, SPE, SLE e SPME). Conceitos básicos de validação. </div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;><strong>Métodos espectrais de análise.</strong> Espectroscopia de absorção molecular no UV-Vis. Espectroscopia de absorção atómica. Atomização por chama, eletrotérmica e indução de plasma. Câmara de vapor frio para análise de mercúrio e gerador de hidretos para determinação de arsénio e selénio.</div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;><strong>Métodos eletroquímicos de análise.</strong> Determinações potenciométricas com elétrodos seletivos de iões. Introdução aos biossensores.</div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;><strong>Métodos cromatográficos.</strong> Fundamentos. Teoria da Cromatografia. Cromatografia em fase gasosa com deteção por ionização de chama, captura de eletrões e espetrometria de massa. # Ficha a avaliar - versão em inglês

## Unidade curricular
Instrumental Methods of Analysis

## Curso responsável
Bachelor in Chemical Engineering

## Créditos ECTS
6

## Ano
2

## Semestre
1

## Objetivos
<p style="text-align: justify;">The main objectives are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Provide an adequate knowledge of the principles, instrumentationand applications of the most commonanalytical techniques,includingatomic and molecular absorption spectroscopy, atomic emission spectroscopy with plasma induction atomization, electrochemical and separative (chromatographic) methods;</li>
<li>Provide an adequate knowledge of the main processes of extraction and handling of solid, liquid and gaseous samples and their implications in terms of analytical measurement uncertainty;</li>
<li>Provide the necessary skillsto enablestudents toselect a particularanalytical techniqueto solveaproblem, determine any restrictions, select themost appropriateanalytical method, identify alternatives, compare the advantagesand disadvantages of eachand develop a critical reasoning to interpret analytical results;</li>
<li>Develop communication skills, particularly of technical results, and group cooperation;</li>
<li>Encourage the use of foundations of scientific theory to solve real-world problems and develop critical thinking.</li>
</ul>

## Resultados de aprendizagem e competências
<p style="text-align: justify;">To achieve these objectives, students should know how to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Explain the principles and mode of operation of the most commonly used analytical equipment in the areas of electrochemistry, spectroscopy and chromatography;</li>
<li>Design an analytic experiment to solve a real problem;</li>
<li>Conduct a laboratory analysis, according to the Good Laboratory Practices, in hygienic and safe conditions, involving sample handling, extraction, pre-concentration and instrumental measurement and calculate the analytical result and its uncertainty;</li>
<li>Interpret and communicate an analytical result and produce technical reports;</li>
<li>Work efficiently in a team.</li>
</ul>

## Pré-requisitos e co-requisitos
Not applicable

## Programa
<div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;><strong><u>Theoretical-practical program</u></strong><strong>:</strong></div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;><strong>Introduction.</strong> Introduction to the Analytical Chemistry and Instrumental Methods of Analysis. Stages of an analytical method. Sample preparation processes (LLE, SPE, SLE and SPME). Basic concepts of validation. </div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;><strong>Spectral methods of analysis.</strong> Molecular absorption spectroscopy in the UV-Vis. Atomic absorption spectroscopy. Flame atomization, electrothermal and plasma induction. Cold vapor chamber for mercury analysis and hydride generator for arsenic and selenium determination.</div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;><strong>Electrochemical methods of analysis.</strong> Potentiometric determination with ion-selective electrodes. Introduction to biosensors.</div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;><strong>Chromatographic methods.</strong> Fundamentals. Chromatography theory. Gas chromatography with flame ionization, electron capture and mass spectrometry detection. High performance liquid chromatography with ultraviolet/visible and fluorescence detection.</div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;><strong><u><br /><br /></u></strong></div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;><strong><u>Laboratory program</u></strong><strong>:</strong></div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;>T0. Validation of an analytical method for the analysis of chlorpyrifos in coffee beans by HPLC-UV;</div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;>T1. Determination of nickel content in an electronic board by flame AAS;</div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;>T2. Validation of an analytical method - Application exercise;</div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;>T3. Determination of peroxide content in an oral elixir by molecular absorption spectroscopy using the standard addition method;</div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;>T4. Determination of nitrates content in a soil by ion selective electrode with potentiometric detection;</div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;>T5. Determination of the concentration of UV-filters in water by HPLC-DAD;</div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;>T6. Determination of the ethanol content in an alcohol gel by GC-FID by internal standard method.</div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;> </div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;>The laboratory classes are held in the laboratory of Instrumental Methods of Analysis of DEQ (E105), with 6 groups per class. Students must complete 7 laboratory works (T0-T6).</div>

## Métodos de ensino e atividades de aprendizagem
<p style="text-align: justify;">Presential classes supported by audiovisual material, emphasizing the application of knowledge to everyday issues and phenomena related to Chemical Engineering and Bioengineering; Resolution of exercises, as well as on-site laboratory classes to carry out laboratory work with analysis and discussion of results.</p>

## Tipo de avaliação
Distributed evaluation without final exam

## Componentes de Avaliação
- Teste: 30.0 %
- Trabalho escrito: 35.0 %
- Trabalho laboratorial: 35.0 %

## Componentes de Ocupação
- Estudo autónomo: 48.0 hours
- Frequência das aulas: 48.0 hours
- Trabalho escrito: 40.0 hours
- Elaboração de relatório/dissertação/tese: 26.0 hours

## Fórmula de cálculo da classificação final
<div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;><br /><p style=&quot;font-weight: 400;&quot;>Final grade (FG) is given by:</p><br /><p style=&quot;font-weight: 400;&quot;><strong>FG = 0.30 MT + 0.35 REL + 0.05 EA + 0.30 EXLAB</strong></p><br /><p style=&quot;font-weight: 400;&quot;>where:</p><br /><p style=&quot;font-weight: 400;&quot;>MT &#8211; average grade of 2 mini-tests of 30 min, scheduled and held during the theoretical-practical classes. Students must obtain a minimum average mark of 7/20. If a student misses any mini-test or does not obtain the minimum mark, he/she will have the opportunity to take an additional mini-test in the last week of classes. If the student does not complete any of the mini-tests or does not obtain a minimum grade, he/she must complete this evaluation component in the special examination period.</p><br /><p style=&quot;font-weight: 400;&quot;>REL - grade of 2 reports of practical work carried out in groups, selected by the teacher, according to the following distribution: 15% Interim report + 20% Final Report.<br /><br />EA - grade of an application exercise carried out during a laboratory lesson, in groups and using an Excel sheet.</p><br /><p style=&quot;font-weight: 400;&quot;>EXLAB &#8211; grade of the written laboratory examination, which will have questions on the work carried out, including calculations referring to the data obtained in each work.</p><br /></div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;> </div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;>Students must reach a minimum grade of 10 out of 20 to complete the course.</div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;> </div><br /><div style=&quot;text-align: justify;&quot;><strong>NOTE: Plagiarism in any written component will be penalized with a zero grade.</strong></div>

## Obtenção de frequência
<p>Laboratory classes are mandatory to all students, including those under article 4, paragraphs a) and b).<br /><br /></p>
<div style="text-align: justify;">To obtain frequency, the students must have carried out all practical works, must have shown the correct preparation and execution of the works, as well as the execution of all necessary calculations and must prepare in the laboratory notebook the discussion of the results obtained, which can be checked by the teacher in the week following its completion.</div>

# Ficha a avaliar - versão em inglês

## Unidade curricular
Theory and Methodology in Information Science

## Curso responsável
Bachelor of Arts in Information Science

## Créditos ECTS
6

## Ano
2

## Semestre
1

## Objetivos
<p>To introduce students to the core or nucleus of Degree in Information Science through a proper understanding of the nature of this science in the wider context of the Humanities and Social Sciences, and its epistemological evolution that is happening in the network environment of Information Era. Provide students with the theoretical assumption of the matrix of the programme and at the same time, make them aware that the IS is an applied social science, i.e., has a clear and applicational vocation, enriching itselvf through a permanent interdisciplinary dynamics.</p>

## Resultados de aprendizagem e competências
<p>Skills: Acquire operational and essential concepts of IS, dominate the understanding of the nature of scientific knowledge generated by IS. Distinguish the evolutionary  and integrated perspective, which is taught and developed at the University of Oporto, from the cumulative or fragmented IS that is taught in many countries of the world. Learn the Quadripolar Method, a method proposed in 1974 for General and Applied Social Sciences and articulate it with the more practical requirements of modeling and to know how to use it in research contexts. Integrate knowledge of other core and complementary courses in the theoretical-methodological matrix inherent to an information scientist.</p>
<p>Results: It is expected that there is a maturity in the reflection process, acquisition of abstract knowledge and the ability to relate different types of information, both theoretical and practical, so that once in the labor market the students know to harmonize the reserach capacity and the critical attitude (scientific) with the instrumental need of solving problems.</p>

## Pré-requisitos e co-requisitos
<p>Not applicable</p>

## Programa
<p>Communication Sciences and Information - genesis, profile and evolution of an interdiscipline in the field of Social Sciences</p>
<p>1. Central epistemological and methodological issues in the social sciences in general and in particular to the CIC</p>
<p>2. From professional practice to scientific training 2.1 Brief retrospective of technical and professional activities related to libraries and archives 2.2 Analysis of a previous distinction: the difference between professional activity and scientific knowledge</p>
<p>3. From Document to Information &#8232; 3.1 Genesis and nuances of basic operational concepts 3.2 Conceptual distinction between Documentation and Information &#8232; &#8232;3.3 Information and its varied polysemy today &#8232; 3.3.1 Information, language and speech &#8232; 3.3.2 Information, knowledge and communication 3.3.3 Information, document and information and communication technologies (ICT)</p>
<p>4. The  epistemological debate &#8232; 4.1 From Paul Otlet to Harol Borko: the crisis of classical library science to boldly programmatic Information Science 4.2 Is Information Science a Science? Different positions and perspectives 4.2.1 The cumulative or fragmented perspective 4.2.2 The evolutionary perspective &#8232; 4.3 The issue of Object - document or information? 4.3.1 The properties of Information 4.3.2 The (im)possibility of Information Science be an interdiscipline 4.3.2.1 The Pluri, interdisciplinarity and transdisciplinarity in the study of information &#8232; 4.3.3 Rebuilding the Object of Information Science from a phenomenological referent and the notion of problem 4.3.4 The info-communicational phenomenon/process 4.3.5 The search of a method in comparison with the existing methodological proposals 4.3.5.1 Scientific methodologies and Quadripolar Method typical of Qualitative Research 4.3.5.2 Theories and models in the theoretical pole &#8232; 4.3.5.2.1 Structuralism and Constructivism 4.3.2.5.2. Systemic theory and  thinking &#8232; 4.3.5.2.3 Theory of Situations and Info-Sense &#8232; &#8232;4.3.5.2.4  Theory of Psychological Information 4.3.5.2.5 Communication Theories 4.3.5.2.6 Different theoretical and practical applications (models)</p>
<p>5. A R&amp;D open programme</p>
<p>6. Information Science is a necessary response to the Information Age and the Internet Galaxy where we are?</p>

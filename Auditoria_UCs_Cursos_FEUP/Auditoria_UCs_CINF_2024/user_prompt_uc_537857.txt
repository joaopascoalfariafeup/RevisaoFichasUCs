## Unidade curricular
Análise de Sistemas de Informação

## Curso responsável
Licenciatura em Ciência da Informação

## Créditos ECTS
6

## Ano
3

## Semestre
1

## Objetivos
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Análise de Sistemas de Informação aborda o ciclo de vida dos sistemas baseados em tecnologias da informação num contexto organizacional: planeamento, conceção, desenvolvimento, implementação, manutenção, eliminação/substituição. <br /><br />Pretende-se que os estudantes desenvolvam um conjunto de competências e conhecimento, tendo como base a gestão da informação, que lhes permita intervir em todas as fases deste ciclo de vida, colaborando com stakeholders de outras áreas disciplinares. O conceito de sistema sócio-técnico é o pano de fundo sobre o qual se abordam metodologias de análise de processos, métodos de modelação conceptual e metodologias de análise e especificação de sistemas.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Pretende-se, então, que dada uma situação de potencial introdução ou desenvolvimento de um sistema de informação numa organização, o estudante seja capaz de participar e/ou conduzir um processo de análise que leve à definição dos requisitos técnicos e organizacionais detalhados desse sistema.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">---------------------------------------- </span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br /></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Competências </span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br /></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">---------------------------------------- </span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br /></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Pretende-se habilitar os alunos com:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">competências técnicas para analisar processos organizacionais e identificar, analisar e especificar requisitos de sistemas de informação;</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">competências transversais para participarem em equipas de projecto envolvidas no desenvolvimento de sistemas de informação nas organizações.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Pretende-se ainda que os alunos adquiram uma atitude sócio-técnica relativa ao desenvolvimento de sistemas de informação nas organizações, isto é, que encarem as actividades de desenvolvimento como requerendo sempre uma intervenção equilibrada e integrada em termos técnicos, organizacionais e sociais.</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br /></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br /></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">---------------------------------------- </span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br /></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Resultados de aprendizagem </span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br /></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">---------------------------------------- </span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br /></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br /></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Sendo aprovados à UC, os estudantes serão capazes de:</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br /><br /></span></p>

<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">explicar o alinhamento entre estratégia de negócio e estratégia de sistemas de informação;</span></li>

<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">explicar como é que a análise dos processos organizacionais é instrumental no ciclo de vida dos sistemas de informação;</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">saber usar métodos de modelação conceptual como apoio à analise de processos organizacionais e à especificação de sistemas de informação.</span></li>

# Ficha a avaliar - versão em inglês

## Unidade curricular
Information Systems Analysis

## Curso responsável
Bachelor of Arts in Information Science

## Créditos ECTS
6

## Ano
3

## Semestre
1

## Objetivos
Information Systems Analysis addresses the life cycle of systems based on information technologies in an organisational context: planning, design, development, implementation, maintenance, elimination/replacement. <br /><br />Students will develop a set of skills and knowledge, whose core is information management, allowing them to intervene in all phases of this life cycle, collaborating with stakeholders from other disciplinary areas. <br /><br />The concept of socio-technical system is the background against which process analysis methodologies, conceptual modelling methods and system analysis and specification methodologies are addressed. <br /><br />Then, given a situation of potential introduction or development of an information system in an organisation, the student will be able to participate and/or conduct an analysis process that leads to the definition of the detailed technical and organisational requirements of that system.

## Resultados de aprendizagem e competências
---------------------------------------- <br />Skills <br />---------------------------------------- <br /><br />This course aims to enable students with: <br /><br /><ul><br /><li>technical skills to analyse organisational processes and identify, analyse and specify information systems requirements;</li><br /><li>transversal skills to participate in project teams involved in the development of information systems in organisations.</li><br /></ul><br />It is also intended that students acquire a socio-technical attitude regarding the development of information systems in organisations, that is, that they face development activities as always requiring a balanced and integrated intervention in technical, organisational and social terms. <br /><br />---------------------------------------- <br />Learning outcomes<br />---------------------------------------- <br /><br />Being approved to the UC, students will be able to: <br /><br /><br /><ol><br /><li>describe the life cycle of technologies and information systems in organisations;</li><br /><li>define what the information systems strategy of an organization is;</li><br /><li>explain the alignment between business strategy and information systems strategy;</li><br /><li>describe the activities involved in the planning and design of information systems;</li><br /><li>explain how the analysis of organisational processes is instrumental in the life cycle of information systems;</li><br /><li>know how to use conceptual modelling methods as support for the analysis of organisational processes and the specification of information systems.</li><br /></ol>

## Programa
<ol><br /><li>Information Systems Planning in organisations</li><br /><li>Analysis of organisational processes for Information Systems</li><br /><li>Information Systems Design</li><br /></ol>

## Métodos de ensino e atividades de aprendizagem
In theoretical classes, the expository method is used to present the conceptual, theoretical and methodological basis of the themes included in the content of the UC. In these classes, the discussion of the UC themes by the students is also carried out, motivated and streamlined by the teacher. <br /><br />In practical classes, the expository teaching method is used for the consolidation of process analysis methodologies, conceptual modelling methods and system analysis and specification methodologies with presentation of examples and exercises. In these classes, through the problem-based method, students also perform small group work based on cases where students apply the knowledge obtained in concrete situations. <br /><br />For monitoring and participation in theoretical and practical classes, students perform readings and previous studies, contained in the UC plan. Due to the practical nature of the discipline, the evaluation is carried out continuously through theoretical/practical work and tests, namely: <br /><br /><br /><ul><br /><li>3 individual tests scheduled at the beginning of the semester;</li><br /><li>3 group work planned at the beginning of the semester.</li><br /></ul>

## Tipo de avaliação
Distributed evaluation without final exam

## Componentes de Avaliação
- Teste: 60.0 %
- Trabalho prático ou de projeto: 40.0 %

## Componentes de Ocupação
- Estudo autónomo: 80.0 hours
- Frequência das aulas: 42.0 hours
- Trabalho de campo: 40.0 hours

## Fórmula de cálculo da classificação final
CF = 0,4*TG + 0,6*MT<br /><br />Minimum grade of 30% required in each test and 40% in the average grade of the tests. Minimum grade of 30% required in each group work and 40% in the average grade of group work.<br /><br />Teachers can differentiate the classification of students from the same group, depending on the perception of the effort and participation developed by each element, as well as participation and discussion in oral presentations in classes.

## Obtenção de frequência
Mandatory presence in 75% of practical classes. Mandatory presence in all tests and scheduled group work.

## Melhoria de classificação
It is possible to improve the grade of the individual component of the tests, at the time of appeal, through an examination. <br /><br />The grade of the group evaluation component is not subject to improvement at the time of appeal. It can only be improved in the next occurrence of the Curricular Unit.

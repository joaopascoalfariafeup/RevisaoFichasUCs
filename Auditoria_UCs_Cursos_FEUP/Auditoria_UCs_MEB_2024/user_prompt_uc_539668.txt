## Objetivos
<p class="p1"><strong>CONTEXTO</strong></p>
<p class="p1">Esta unidade curricular está centrada na aplicação de métodos analíticos para tomar melhores decisões e fornece aos estudantes ferramentas de modelação e de otimização que serão de grande utilidade na abordagem e resolução de problemas das organizações (indústria e serviços). Salienta igualmente a importância da sustentabilidade, Enfatiza ainda a importância da sustentabilidade, integrando os Objectivos de Desenvolvimento Sustentável (ODS) das Nações Unidas, de forma a garantir que as soluções propostas contribuem para um futuro mais sustentável e equitativo.<br /><br /><strong>OBJETIVOS ESPECÍFICOS</strong></p>
<p class="p2">O objetivo principal desta unidade curricular é, através da criação de modelos, desenvolver competências para análise de um conjunto vasto de situações reais. Essas competências baseiam-se na capacidade de reconhecer o problema-chave numa situaçãonão estruturada, na capacidade de desenvolver uma estrutura para analisar a tratar o problema e na aplicação de métodos analíticos na sua resolução.</p>
<p class="p1"><strong>OBJETIVOS DE APRENDIZAGEM</strong></p>
<p class="p1">Dotar os estudantes com competências para:<br /> <br />- identificar e abordar de forma hábil e estruturada problemas de decisão;<br />- construir modelos de problemas de decisão;<br />- integrar considerações de sustentabilidade nos modelos de decisão e alinhar as soluções propostas com os Objectivos de Desenvolvimento Sustentável (ODS);<br />- identificar e recorrer a métodos analíticos para obtenção de soluções para os modelos construídos, como suporte para decisões fundamentadas;<br />- usar ferramentas informáticas para análise e obtenção de soluções para os modelos construídos;<br />- extrair informação dos modelos e utilizá-la para comunicar e motivar mudanças organizacionais.</p>

<p class="MsoNormal">Esta unidade curricular contribuirá para a aquisição, por parte dos estudantes, das seguintes competências, de acordo com a nomenclatura CDIO:<br /><br />1. Conhecimento Técnico e Raciocínio<br /><br />1.1. Adquirir com a necessária proficiência conhecimentos de ciências básicas e ser capaz de os utilizar na formulação, resolução e discussão de problemas da sua área de formação;<br /><br />Os conteúdos da unidade curricular constroem sobre conhecimentos de Álgebra, Análise Matemática e Probabilidades e Estatística, organizando-os, desenvolvendo-os e aplicando-os num contexto de apoio à resolução de problemas de decisão. Por natureza estes conhecimentos são transversais a todas as áreas de Engenharia e pertencem às suas ciências básicas. Durante as aulas há a preocupação de usar exemplos e casos da área científica do curso onde os estudantes estão envolvidos, fazendo a ponte entre as ciências básicas e a área de formação específica do curso.<br />Para além disso os estudantes são incentivados a integrar nas suas análises e formulações de problemas considerações sobre sustentabilidade e alinhadas com os ODS.<br /><br />Todos os elementos de avaliação usados na unidade curricular endereçam a avaliação destas competências.<br /><br />2. Capacidades e Atitudes Pessoais e Profissionais<br /><br />2.1. Raciocínio em engenharia e resolução de problemas<br />2.1.1 identificação e formulação de problemas<br />2.1.2 modelização<br />2.1.3 estimação e análise qualitativa<br />2.1.4 análise com incerteza<br />2.1.5 solução e recomendação<br /><br />A metodologia de ensino e os seus conteúdos podem ser descritos precisamente por estes pontos. Assim, a formulação de problemas de acordo com cada um dos modelos estudados ao longo da unidade curricular, a sua modelização e resolução são uma atividade permanente ao longo da aprendizagem. ## Objetivos
<p class="p1"><strong>BACKGROUND</strong></p>
<p class="p1">This course focuses on the application of analytical methods to make better decisions and provides students with tools for modelling and optimization that will be very useful in various roles in several types of organizations (industry and services). It also emphasizes the importance of sustainability by integrating the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) to ensure that the proposed solutions contribute to a more sustainable and equitable future.</p>
<p class="p1"><strong>SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES</strong></p>
<p class="p1">The main objective of this course is, through the creation of models, to develop skills for analysing a wide range of real situations. These competencies are based on the ability to recognize the key problem in a non-structured situation, on the ability todevelop a framework for analysing and treating the problem and on the application of analytical methods for its resolution.</p>
<p class="p1"><strong>LEARNING OBJECTIVES</strong></p>
<p class="p1">Endow the students with the skills to:</p>
- identify and address decision problems in a structured way;<br />- build models of decision problems;<br />- integrate sustainability considerations into decision models and align proposed solutions with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs);<br />- identify and use analytical methods to obtain solutions for the models, that should act as a support for informed decisions;<br />- use software packages to analyse and obtain solutions for the models;<br />- extract information from the models to communicate and motivate organizational changes.<br />

## Resultados de aprendizagem e competências
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-margin-top-alt: auto; mso-margin-bottom-alt: auto;">This course will contribute to the acquisition, by the students, of the following CDIO competencies:<br /><br />1. Technical Knowledge and Reasoning<br /><br />1.1. Acquire proficiency with the necessary knowledge of basic sciences and be able to use them in the formulation, discussion and resolution of problems of their area;<br /><br />The contents of the course unit build on the knowledge of Algebra, Mathematical Analysis and Probabilities and Statistics, organizing them, developing them and applying them in a context of decision support. By nature, this knowledge is transversal to all areas of Engineering and belongs to its basic sciences. Throughout the classes there is a concern to use examples and case studies from the scientific area of each course, bridging the gap between the basic sciences and the area of the degree.<br />In addition, students are encouraged to integrate sustainability and SDG-aligned considerations into their analysis and problem formulation.<br /><br />The assessment in the course addresses these skills.<br /><br />2. Personal and Professional Skills and Attitudes<br /><br />2.1. Engineering reasoning and problem-solving<br />2.1.1 problem identification and formulation<br />2.1.2 modelling<br />2.1.3 estimation and qualitative analysis<br />2.1.4 analysis with uncertainty<br />2.1.5 solution and recommendation<br /><br />Exactly these points can describe the teaching methodology and the contents of this course. Thus, the formulation of problems according to each of the models studied throughout the course, their modelling and resolution are an ongoing activity. The qualitative analysis is addressed in the sensitivity analysis, while uncertainty is addressed in Queueing Theory and in Decision Theory.<br /><br />The assessment in the course addresses these skills.<br /><br />2.2 Experimentation and knowledge discovery<br /><br />2.2.1 hypothesizing<br />2.2.2 literature search<br />2.2.3 Experimental investigation<br />2.2.4 test and defence of hypotheses<br /><br />2.3 systemic thinking<br /><br />2.3.1 holistic thinking<br />2.3.2 emergency and interaction between systems<br />2.3.3 prioritization and focus<br />2.3.4 trade-offs, judgment and balancing the resolution<br /><br />These skills are addressed indirectly and maybe more directly described as:<br /><br />(1) analysis competencies - ability to conceptualize, formulate and solve unfamiliar problems;<br />(2) project competencies - ability to address with creativity, unfamiliar, complex and technically uncertain problems;<br />(3) research competencies - ability to model and evaluate the applicability of emerging techniques (description of the criteria according to Euro-ACE).<br /><br />Problem design and modelling, involving the identification of the applicable family of models and the development and evaluation of original models, clearly contributes to these skills. The topics related to sensitivity analysis contribute to the understanding of concepts related to trade-offs and balancing.<br /><br />Students will be encouraged to consider the implications of the decisions in terms of sustainability, specifically the impact of their models and solutions on areas such as health, education, equality, and the preservation of the environment.<br /><br />The assessment in the course addresses these skills.<br /><br />The learning objectives are related to the competencies (knowledge, skills and attitudes that students should have) and are expressed as statements about things that students should be able to explain, calculate, deduct, design, etc ...<br /><br />For all program topics, detailed learning objectives are available from the website, along with educational materials to support learning.</p>

## Métodos de ensino e atividades de aprendizagem
<p>The themes are presented using active learning methods. The problems are illustrated with examples. The problems from a list of proposed problems are discussed and solved by the students.</p>

Distributed evaluation with final exam

- Teste: 70.0 %

- Estudo autónomo: 110.0 hours

## Fórmula de cálculo da classificação final
<strong>DISTRIBUTED EVALUATION</strong><br /><br /><p>Micro-exercises in class (closed book) (0 to 10 points)<br /><br />The sum of the scores obtained by each student in the assessment exercises proposed for resolution at the end of each lesson, removing the two lowest scores.<br /><br />Each exercise will be rated on a scale from 0 to 100%.<br /><br />Test (closed book) (0 to 4 points).<br /><br />Distributed evaluation: final rate between 0 and 14 points.</p><br /><p><strong>EXAM</strong> (open book) (0 to 6 points).</p>

## Obtenção de frequência
To be able to access the exam the students must obtain a minimum classification of 4 points (out of 14 possible points) in the distributed assessment component (micro-exercises and intermediate test).<br /><br />In addition to the previous requirement, to attend this curricular unit students must follow FEUP general rules of assessment (in Portuguese https://paginas.fe.up.pt/~contqf/producao/_SERAC/Legislacao/Regulamentos/RegulamentosFEUP/normas%20gerais%20de%20avaliacao.pdf)

## Melhoria de classificação
<p>Students may register for completion, in the Appeal Season, of each of the different components of assessment:<br /><br />1. The component - exercises - will be assessed through a closed book test (classification from 0 to 10 values).<br /><br />2. The component - test - will be assessed by a closed-book test (classification of 0 to 4).<br /><br />3. The component - exam - will be assessed by means of a closed book exam (classification from 0 to 6).<br /><br />In all cases the final mark will be calculated as described in the section "Calculation of the Final Classification", considering the best assessment obtained by the student in each component, considering the normal season and the improvement season.</p>

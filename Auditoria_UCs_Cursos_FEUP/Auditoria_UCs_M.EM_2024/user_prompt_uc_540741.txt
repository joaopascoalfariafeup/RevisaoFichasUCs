# Ficha a avaliar - versão em português

## Unidade curricular
Simulação de Processos Tecnológicos

## Curso responsável
Mestrado em Engenharia Mecânica

## Créditos ECTS
4,5

## Ano
1

## Semestre
2

## Objetivos
<p data-start="0" data-end="318">Aquisição de conhecimentos que possibilitem a implementação, compreensão e análise de modelação numérica de Processos Tecnológicos e, em particular, a modelação de Processos de Conformação Plástica (em Massa e em Chapas Metálicas). Espera-se que, no final do período letivo, os estudantes:</p>
<ol data-start="320" data-end="1147" data-is-last-node="">
<li data-start="320" data-end="492"><strong data-start="323" data-end="335">Conheçam</strong> os fundamentos dos métodos utilizados nos programas de modelação numérica a serem aplicados: Método das Diferenças Finitas e Método dos Elementos Finitos.</li>
<li data-start="493" data-end="640"><strong data-start="496" data-end="515">Saibam utilizar</strong> as ferramentas de pré-processamento, análise e pós-processamento necessárias à simulação numérica dos processos em estudo.</li>
<li data-start="641" data-end="1147" data-is-last-node=""><strong data-start="644" data-end="661">Sejam capazes</strong> de criar o modelo numérico de casos práticos de componentes obtidos por diferentes processos de fabrico, efetuar a sua análise e obter resultados que lhes permitam desenvolver um sentido crítico sobre a produção do componente. Devem igualmente ser capazes de analisar esses resultados, identificar os defeitos que o componente tende a apresentar, conhecer a relação desses defeitos com as variáveis do processo e propor caminhos para a resolução dos problemas e otimização do processo.</li>
</ol>

# Ficha a avaliar - versão em inglês

## Unidade curricular
Simulation of Technological Processes

## Curso responsável
Master in Mechanical Engineering

## Créditos ECTS
4,5

## Ano
1

## Semestre
2

## Objetivos
<p>Specific objectives: Acquisition of knowledge that will enable the implementation, understanding and analysis of numerical modelling of Technological Processes and in particular the modelling of Plastic Forming processes (in Mass and in Metal Sheets). It is expected that, at the end of the academic period, students will<br /> 1. Know the fundamentals of the methods used in the numerical modelling programmes to be used: Finite Difference Method and Finite Element Method.<br /> 2. Know how to use the pre-processing, analysis and post-processing tools required for the numerical simulation of the processes to be studied. <br />3. Be able to create the numerical model of practical cases of components obtained by different manufacturing processes, carry out their analysis and obtain the results that will allow them to have a critical mind about the production of the component. They should be able to analyse the results obtained, define the defects that the component tends to present, know the relationship of these defects with the process variables and estimate a path for solving the problems and optimising the process.</p>

## Resultados de aprendizagem e competências
<p><span style="font-family: Helvetica; font-size: 12px;"><span style="vertical-align: inherit;">It is expected that, at the end of the academic period, students will have developed: </span></span></p>
<p style="margin: 0px; font-size: 12px; font-family: Helvetica;"><span style="vertical-align: inherit;">- knowledge of different numerical methods used to model technological processes, their characteristics and respective advantages and limitations;</span></p>
<p style="margin: 0px; font-size: 12px; font-family: Helvetica;"><span style="vertical-align: inherit;">- the ability to carry out all the steps necessary to perform numerical simulation of different technological processes;<br /><br /></span></p>
<p style="margin: 0px; font-size: 12px; font-family: Helvetica;"><span style="vertical-align: inherit;">- the ability to characterize metallic materials, define the most appropriate constitutive equations for each material and obtain the respective parameters based on the results of existing experimental tests;</span></p>
<p style="margin: 0px; font-size: 12px; font-family: Helvetica;"><span style="vertical-align: inherit;">- ability to analyze and discuss the numerical results obtained and relate them to both numerical and process parameters in order to optimize the desired results;<br /><br /></span></p>
<p style="margin: 0px; font-size: 12px; font-family: Helvetica;"><span style="vertical-align: inherit;">- ability to integrate knowledge from various areas of mechanical engineering, such as numerical methods, solid mechanics, behavior and characterization of materials, manufacturing processes, among others;</span></p>
<p style="margin: 0px; font-size: 12px; font-family: Helvetica;"><span style="vertical-align: inherit;">- ability to work in a group and flexibility to work with different peers, ability to express yourself in writing and present orally.</span></p>
<p><span style="vertical-align: inherit;"><span style="vertical-align: inherit;"> </span></span></p>
<p> </p>

# Ficha a avaliar - versão em inglês

## Unidade curricular
International Negotiation

## Curso responsável
Master in Innovation and Technological Entrepreneurship

## Créditos ECTS
3

## Ano
1

## Semestre
1

## Objetivos
<p style="font-weight: 400;">Introduce the detailed study and analysis of the negotiation process in multicultural environments (beyond the international environment): </p>
<ul>
<li>Identity, categorise and understand the conflict at the origin of negotiation, and which conditions the negotiation process; </li>
<li>Identify and understand the interactions of cultural and ethical values conditioning decision-making throughout the negotiation process;</li>
<li>Identify and understand the specificities of each phase of the negotiation process.</li>
</ul>
<span style="font-weight: 400;">I</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">ntroduce the detailed study and analysis of the ethical requirements set out in European legislation on anti-corruption and transparency and their interactions associated with decision-making in management, particularly throughout the negotiation process. <br />To develop the ability to analyse different situations critically and to define and implement actions throughout the phases of the negotiation process (from preparation to follow-up of agreements). </span>

## Resultados de aprendizagem e competências
<p style="font-weight: 400;">In the end, participants will be able to</p>
<ul>
<li>Identify conflicts and develop actions to manage decision-making processes, and in particular those to mitigate the cultural and ethical impacts of non-ethical issues and safeguard them;</li>
<li>Identify and implement the ethical constraints (in particular those resulting from the European legislation in force) in decision-making;</li>
<li>Identify cultural aspects and develop processes to mitigate their impacts on the negotiation process;</li>
<li>Design the strategy, planning, preparation and participation in negotiation processes in a multicultural environment (with a p focus on the development of a win-win methodology);</li>
<li>Design a negotiation process in an international or multicultural environment.</li>
</ul>

## Pré-requisitos e co-requisitos
does not apply

## Programa
<p>1 From local to global, from national to international, multiculturalism. The decision-making process. Conflict, ethics and culture are the perfect ingredients of the explosive cocktail in decision-making and negotiation. (Inter)national or (multi)cultural negotiation?</p>
<p>2. Conflict. Types, sources, development and management of conflict. From intra and interpersonal to intra and intergroup conflicts. The role of judgement in the search for conflict resolution. Agreement or consensus.</p>
<p>3. Culture: Values, models and dimensions. Inter- and cross-cultural impacts. The impact of culture on decision-making and the negotiation process.</p>
<p>4. Ethics: Values and ethical dilemmas. The phenomenon of "ethical blindness". Toxic manager. Ethical issues in business (European directives and their consequences). </p>
<p>5. Negotiation. Strategy, type, process and phases of negotiation. The ethical and cultural impacts on the negotiation process. The mitigation of these impacts.</p>
<p>6. Overview of the interconnections: negotiation-culture-ethics. Case studies and examples. Wrap-up</p>
<p style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>Note:<br /></strong> In addition to the examples presented in each of the theoretical presentations, a case study will be used, which will be analysed in each of the sessions, according to the issues addressed therein.</p>

## Métodos de ensino e atividades de aprendizagem
<p style="font-weight: 400;">The methodology employed is based on a combination of the following approaches:</p>
<ul>
<li>Presentation of theoretical concepts;</li>
<li>Analysis of situations that exemplify the theoretical concepts;</li>
<li>Identification and analysis of day-to-day situations;</li>
<li>Discussion on impacts and ways of mitigating them.</li>
<li>Detailed analysis of a case study regarding the interactions of the various concepts;</li>
</ul>

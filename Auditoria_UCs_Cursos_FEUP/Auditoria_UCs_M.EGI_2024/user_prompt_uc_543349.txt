# Ficha a avaliar - versão em inglês

## Unidade curricular
Maintenance and Asset Management

## Curso responsável
Master in Industrial Engineering and Management

## Créditos ECTS
6

## Ano
1

## Semestre
2

## Objetivos
<p>The effectiveness of the Maintenance of a Production Plant lies in its ability of assuring a certain required level of technical availability, and optimizing the entrepreneurial productivity and flexibility and the product&#8217;s quality itself. All the maintenance actions shall comply with defined strategic company goals andorganizational schemes.</p>
<p>The aim of this curricular unit is to provide students with an integrated overall view of the most common concepts, techniques and strategies used in Asset Management. A decision support system for maintenance is presented in order to allow students to solve the main maintenance management problems and to help them make the best decisions in the scope of Asset Management.</p>

## Resultados de aprendizagem e competências
<p class="MsoNormal"><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: 'Trebuchet MS', sans-serif;">At the end of the semester, students should be capable of:</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: 'Trebuchet MS', sans-serif;">- </span><span lang="EN-US" style="text-indent: -18pt; font-size: 9pt; font-family: 'Trebuchet MS', sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: 7pt; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"> </span></span><span lang="EN-US" style="text-indent: -18pt; font-size: 9pt; font-family: 'Trebuchet MS', sans-serif;">Understand the role of Asset Management for the competitiveness of companies; have a global and integrated view of maintenance management (various types of maintenance, associated costs, planning and control).</span></p>
<p class="MsoListParagraph" style="margin-left: 18.0pt; mso-add-space: auto; text-indent: -18.0pt; mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1;"> <span style="font-family: 'Trebuchet MS', sans-serif; font-size: 9pt;">- Analyse the actual or expected reliability of a product, process or service, identifying the actions to reduce the failures and mitigate their effect;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: 'Trebuchet MS', sans-serif;">- Acquire competences in using tools and techniques for the lifetime analysis of systems;<br /><br />- Acquire competences in optimization techniques applied to asset management</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: 'Trebuchet MS', sans-serif;">- Acquire detailed knowledge of Total Productive Maintenance (TPM)</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: 'Trebuchet MS', sans-serif;">- Understand the critical factors of success when implementing TPM plans;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 9pt; font-family: 'Trebuchet MS', sans-serif;">- Use tools and methodologies to support TPM, such as Kobetsu Kaizen, Autonomous Maintenance and Planned Maintenance.<br /><br /></span></p>

## Programa
<p><br />Maintenance Organisation and Maintenance Techniques. <br />Planning of maintenance activities; Maintenance costs; Maintenance Types: KPIs of efficiency.<br /> Analyze the current or expected reliability of a product, process or service, identifying actions to reduce the occurrence of malfunctions and mitigate their effects.<br /> Reliability modelling compounding &#8220;Engineer-driven&#8221; and &#8220;Data-driven&#8221; methods. Reliability Growth. Bathtube curve Reliability Testing and Management Reliability Block Diagrams.<br />Predictive Maintenance. Monitoring, simulation and optimization of assets&#8217; condition.<br />Condition monitoring. Discrete and continuous models.<br />ETA, FTA; FMEA and FMECA.<br /> Estimating the assets Remaining Useful Life (RUL).<br /> Identify the factors (internal or external, controllable or non-controllable) that influence the assets&#8217; behaviour.<br /> Reliability Centered-Maintenance.<br /> Optimization of maintenance strategy, management of spare parts and equipment replacement policies Total Productive Maintenance (TPM) </p>

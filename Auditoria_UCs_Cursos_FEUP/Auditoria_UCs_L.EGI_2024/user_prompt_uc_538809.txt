## Objetivos
<p class="p1"><strong>CONTEXTO</strong></p>
<p class="p1">Esta unidade curricular centra-se na aplicação de métodos analíticos para uma melhor tomada de decisão e fornece aos estudantes ferramentas de modelação e otimização que serão extremamente úteis na abordagem e resolução de problemas organizacionais. Para além disso, aborda conceitos relacionados com a gestão da incerteza em vários contextos organizacionais (indústria e serviços). Enfatiza ainda a importância da sustentabilidade, integrando os Objectivos de Desenvolvimento Sustentável (ODS) das Nações Unidas, de forma a garantir que as soluções propostas contribuem para um futuro mais sustentável e equitativo.</p>
<p class="p1"><strong>OBJETIVOS ESPECÍFICOS</strong></p>
<p class="p2">O objetivo principal desta unidade curricular é desenvolver competências para analisar uma vasta gama de situações do mundo real através da criação de modelos. Estas competências baseiam-se na identificação do problema-chave numa situação não estruturada, no desenvolvimento de um quadro para o analisar e abordar, e na aplicação de métodos analíticos para o resolver.</p>
<p class="p1"><strong>OBJETIVOS DE APRENDIZAGEM</strong></p>
Dotar os estudantes das competências necessárias para:<br />
<ul>
<li>Identificar e abordar problemas de decisão de uma forma hábil e estruturada;</li>
<li>Construir modelos de problemas de decisão;</li>
<li>Integrar considerações de sustentabilidade nos modelos de decisão e alinhar as soluções propostas com os Objectivos de Desenvolvimento Sustentável (ODS);</li>
<li>Identificar e utilizar métodos analíticos para obter soluções para os modelos construídos para apoiar decisões informadas;</li>
<li>Utilizar ferramentas computacionais para analisar e obter soluções para os modelos construídos;</li>
<li>Extrair informação dos modelos e utilizá-la para comunicar e motivar a mudança organizacional.</li>
</ul>

## Objetivos
<p><strong>BACKGROUND</strong></p>
<p>This course focuses on the application of analytical methods fo</p>
<p>better decision-making and provides students with modeling</p>
<p>and optimization tools that will be highly useful in addressing</p>
<p>and solving organizational problems. In addition, it covers</p>
<p>concepts related to uncertainty management in various</p>
<p>organizational contexts. It also emphasizes the importance of</p>
<p>sustainability by integrating the United Nations Sustainable</p>
<p>Development Goals (SDGs) to ensure that the proposed</p>
<p>solutions contribute to a more sustainable and equitable future</p>
<p><strong>SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES</strong></p>
<p>The main objective of this course is to develop skills for</p>
<p>analyzing a wide range of real-world situations by creating</p>
<p>models. These skills are based on identifying the key problem i</p>
<p>an unstructured situation, developing a framework to analyze</p>
<p>and address it, and applying analytical methods to solve it.</p>
<p><strong>LEARNING OBJECTIVES</strong></p>
<p>Provide students with the skills to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Identify and address decision problems in a skillful and structured manner;</li>
<li>Build models of decision problems;</li>
<li>Integrate sustainability considerations into decision models and align proposed solutions with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs);</li>
<li>Identify and use analytical methods to obtain solutions to the constructed models to support informed decisions</li>
<li>Use computational tools to analyze and obtain solutions for the constructed models;</li>
<li>Extract information from the models and use it to communicate and motivate organizational change.</li>
</ul>

## Resultados de aprendizagem e competências
<p class="p1">This course will contribute to the acquisition, by the students, of the following CDIO competencies:</p>
<p class="p2"><strong> 1. Technical Knowledge and Reasoning</strong></p>
<p class="p1">1.1. Acquire proficiency with the necessary knowledge of basic sciences and be able to use them in the formulation, discussion and resolution of problems of their area;</p>
<p class="p1">The contents of the course unit build on the knowledge of Algebra, Mathematical Analysis and Probabilities and Statistics, organizing them, developing them and applying them in a context of decision support. By nature, this knowledge is transversal to all areas of Engineering and belongs to its basic sciences. Throughout the classes there is a concern to use examples and case studies from the scientific area of each course, bridging the gap between the basic sciences and the area of the degree.<br />In addition, students are encouraged to integrate sustainability and SDG-aligned considerations into their analysis and problem formulation.</p>
<p class="p1">The assessment in the course addresses these skills.</p>
<p class="p1"><strong>2. Personal and Professional Skills and Attitudes </strong></p>
<p class="p1">2.1. Engineering reasoning and problem-solving</p>

<div class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;">2.1.2 modelling</div>
<div class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;">2.1.3 estimation and qualitative analysis</div>
<div class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;">2.1.4 analysis with uncertainty</div>
<div class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;">2.1.5 solution and recommendation</div>
<p class="p1">Exactly these points can describe the teaching methodology and the contents of this course. Thus, the formulation of problems according to each of the models studied throughout the course, their modelling and resolution are an ongoing activity. The qualitative analysis is addressed in the sensitivity analysis, while uncertainty is addressed in Queueing Theory and in Decision Theory.</p>
<p class="p1">The assessment in the course addresses these skills.<br /><br />Students will be encouraged to consider the implications of the decisions in terms of sustainability, specifically the impact of their models and solutions on areas such as health, education, equality, and the preservation of the environment.</p>
<p class="p1">2.2 Experimentation and knowledge discovery</p>
<div class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;">2.2.1 hypothesizing</div>
<div class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;">2.2.2 literature search</div>

<div class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;">2.2.4 test and defence of hypotheses</div>
<p class="p1">2.3 systemic thinking</p>
<div class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;">2.3.1 holistic thinking</div>
<div class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;">2.3.2 emergency and interaction between systems</div>
<div class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;">2.3.3 prioritization and focus</div>
<div class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;">2.3.4 trade-offs, judgment and balancing the resolution</div>
<p class="p1">These skills are addressed indirectly and maybe more directly described as:</p>
<div class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;">(1) analysis competencies - ability to conceptualize, formulate and solve unfamiliar problems;</div>
<div class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;">(2) project competencies - ability to address with creativity, unfamiliar, complex and technically uncertain problems;</div>
<div class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;">(3) research competencies - ability to model and evaluate the applicability of emerging techniques (description of the criteria according to Euro-ACE).</div>
<p class="p1">Problem design and modelling, involving the identification of the applicable family of models and the development and evaluation of original models, clearly contributes to these skills. The topics related to sensitivity analysis contribute to the understanding of concepts related to trade-offs and balancing.</p>
<p class="p1">The assessment in the course addresses these skills.</p>
<p class="p1">The learning objectives are related to the competencies (knowledge, skills and attitudes that students should have) and are expressed as statements about things that students should be able to explain, calculate, deduct, design, etc ...</p>
